Solver Liquidity — The Fulfillment Engine

Solvers are specialized off-chain agents that monitor the SPECTRO intent mempool to identify and fulfill user requests. By providing immediate liquidity on the destination chain, solvers abstract away the waiting times typical of traditional cross-chain bridges.

Role of the Solver

  • Instant Fulfillment: Solvers use their own capital to satisfy the user's intent immediately, assuming the short-term bridging risk.
  • Efficient Routing: They compete to provide the most cost-effective execution, ensuring users receive the best possible rates.
  • Technical Execution: Solvers call the executeIntent function, providing the necessary proofs and the user's EIP-712 signature.

Incentive Mechanism

Solvers are motivated by a Protocol Fee defined within the WithdrawalIntent. Once an intent is successfully proven and fulfilled on the target chain, the SpectroCore contract releases the original funds plus the fee to the solver's address.

Operational Requirements

  1. High Liveness: Solvers must maintain constant uptime to catch and fulfill intents before they expire.
  2. Capital Management: Adequate liquidity across multiple chains is required to compete effectively in the network.
  3. Proof Generation: Solvers are responsible for monitoring source chain states to trigger the fulfillCondition logic when required.
  4. Gas Optimization: Using advanced execution strategies to minimize on-chain costs during the fulfillment process.

Trustless Settlement

The SPECTRO Protocol ensures that solvers cannot claim funds without providing valid cryptographic proof of fulfillment. The provenConditions mapping acts as a decentralized ledger, protecting both the user's assets and the solver's rewards.

Solver Risks

Solvers face risks such as chain reorgs or failed proof validations. To mitigate this, SPECTRO implements strict validation checks and supports decentralized oracles for state verification.
