Fraud Protection — Safeguarding the Intent Ecosystem

Security in the SPECTRO Protocol is built on cryptographic certainty. By combining off-chain signatures with on-chain verification, we ensure that every intent is fulfilled exactly as defined, protecting both users from theft and solvers from non-payment.

Deterministic Nonces

To prevent **Replay Attacks**, SPECTRO implements a strict nonce management system. Each intent is assigned a unique identifier that is marked as "used" immediately upon execution, ensuring that a single signature cannot be broadcast twice across the network.

Cryptographic State Proofs

  • Verifiable Fulfillment: Solvers must provide proof that the destination chain state matches the user's intent parameters before the settlement is finalized.
  • Trustless Escrow: Funds are held securely in the SpectroCore contract and are only released when the cryptographic conditions are satisfied.
  • Data Integrity: EIP-712 signatures ensure that the intent data cannot be tampered with by intermediaries or solvers.

Common Vulnerability Mitigations

  • Reentrancy Protection: Our contracts utilize gas-optimized guards to prevent malicious actors from draining pools through recursive calls.
  • Access Control: Administrative functions are restricted to verified roles, ensuring that the protocol's core logic remains immutable and secure.
  • Type-Safe Logic: Leveraging the **Solady** library to prevent numerical overflows and ensure efficient, secure math operations.

User Security Guidelines

  1. Signature Verification: Always inspect the EIP-712 popup in your wallet to confirm the targetChain and amount.
  2. Deadline Management: Use short-lived deadlines for urgent transfers to limit the window of exposure for signed intents.
  3. Official Endpoints: Only interact with the protocol through verified RPC providers and the official SPECTRO dashboard.

Infrastructure Hardening

SPECTRO monitors the solver network for malicious behavior. Any attempt to submit invalid proofs results in immediate transaction rejection by the smart contract, maintaining the overall health of the liquidity pools.
